The Story Behind The Bodyweight Gym

From Scientist to Calisthenics Coach

I have a background in both marine science and software engineering. After working as a scientist, I left to pursue coaching and sport full-time. What followed was a humbling journey of failure, persistence, and breakthrough.

This is what I learned — the hard way.

The Journey

Every Expert Was Once a Beginner

I opened The Bodyweight Gym in 2017 — a calisthenics studio in Perth, Australia. For four years, I coached everyone from complete beginners to competitive athletes.

In the fitness industry, two skills immediately set you apart as a trainer: a strict ring muscle-up and a freestanding handstand. So that's what I learned to teach.

Problem was, I had no gymnastics background. I was a 30-something adult learning from scratch.

The first 12 months nearly broke me. Elbow tendonitis that wouldn't go away because I kept progressing too fast. Muscles ready, tendons weren't. I learned the hard way what most programmes ignore: muscles adapt in ~3 months, but tendons and ligaments take ~9 months.

It took two years of trial and error, countless failed attempts, and one breakthrough moment when someone asked me to show them something on the rings I'd installed in my apartment. That night, I nailed my first strict ring muscle-up.

Philosophy

What I Believe

I learned everything the hard way so you don't have to. These principles guide every programme I create:

1

Graduation Before Progression

Each level has a concrete test. You don't move on until your body proves it's ready.

2

Respect Connective Tissue

Muscles adapt faster than tendons. Training by how your muscles feel will get you injured.

3

Strict Movement

No kipping, no momentum tricks. Build real strength that transfers to everything else.

4

No Fluff, No Shortcuts

Just progressive overload, periodisation, and patience. The basics work.
